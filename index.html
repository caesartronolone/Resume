Hosting a panel discussion

My criteria for choosing speakers were as follows: a diverse group (both in background and also investments), thoughtful and willingness to expand on answers, track record of sharing knowledge (podcasts, blogs, social media), respected, and compelling speakers.

The top 3 shown below all pass these checks with flying colors. They all bring tremendous experience outside of fundraisng and are able to leverage those skills into being successful investors. I think that is an important part of recognizing your edge as an investor and also important to me as I hope my skills can bring unique value to the goingVC team. In the description of each person I aim to list how they exceed these critera for the panel

To reach out, I would first email them and their team inviting them to the panel. I would also reach out by phone or through networking chanels if email was not sucessfull. After receiving the invite, I would share the questions in advance to allow them to be most prepared. Although I would also have some follow up questions prepared to keep the panel discussion lively and prevent it from becoming too scripted.

Top 3 VC Choices

1. Bill Gurley - Benchmark Captial

Started out as a design engineer in computer hardware at Compaq Computers and technical marketing for AMD. He was then a research analyst on Wall St. He has been in venture capital as the GP at Benchmark since 1999 - which has had successful investments in Uber, Grubhub, Zillow, OpenTable, Stitchfix, and many more. He has a blog and wrote a book on VC at Benchmark Capital, he also co-hosts a bi-weekly podcast with fellow VC Brad Gershner called BG2

2. Naval Ravikant - Hit Forge and others

Started at BCG after college and founded multiple companies since then. Early investor in Uber, FourSquare, Twitter, Wish.com, Poshmark, Postmates, Thumbtack, Notion, Opendoor, Clubhouse, Stack Overflow, and Yammer, with over 70 total exits and more than 10 Unicorn companies. Has his own podcast caled Nav.al and is featured on many other podcasts. His famous twitter thread on Hapiness was curated and published in a free downloadable book

3. Sheryl Sandberg - Sandberg Bernthal Venture Partners

Sheryl started at Mckinsey, then worked in the dept of the US treasury. She later joined Google in 2001 responsible for online sales. Then famously joined Facebook as COO in 2008 where she transitioned the company into a profitable business. She started a venture fund in 2021 with her husband called Sandberg Bernthal Venture Partners which is invested in Pigment and Cercle. She is newer to the VC space but has tremendous experience in driving profit in the biggest busnesses (and government) and can bring a different perspective due to her newer outlook and learnings She has written 2 books and recently released a documentary on the conflict in the middle east

Questions to Ask

Starting a Fund

What is the first thing you do when deciding to create a new fund?

What are the things you believe if you get right, you will have a successful fund?

How to recruit LPs

How to identify and recruit LPs?

Do you reach out to an existing network? If so can you talk to the early days of being in VC

How selective are you? Do you look for certain alignments?

How to build their teams:

How to structure your teams

What types of employees are you looking for?

What are the most important processes to instill in the team?

Maintaining the fund:

How to manage LP relationships throughout the fund?

Do you ever have investments that are questionable whether they fit into your fund objectives? How do you manage deciding if it is a good fit?
